About Dr. David Bennett, DVM

A Walnut Creek native bringing decades of clinical experience to East Bay families — one home visit at a time.

Bennett Veterinary Service (formerly Dr. Bennett’s Veterinary House Calls) continues to provide compassionate in-home pet euthanasia and mobile veterinary care throughout the East Bay.

A Lifelong Love of Animals

Dr. David Bennett was born and raised in Walnut Creek, California. From an early age it was clear which direction his life would take — he began collecting bugs and catching snakes at age seven, and during high school he and his brother Mathew worked in wildlife rehabilitation, caring for orphaned and injured birds of prey.

Education and Training

Dr. Bennett earned a Bachelor of Science in Microbiology and Medical Technology from California Polytechnic State University in San Luis Obispo in 1988. After spending five years working in pharmaceuticals and education, he returned to his lifelong calling and graduated from the UC Davis School of Veterinary Medicine as a Doctor of Veterinary Medicine in 1997.

A Practice Built on Compassion

Dr. Bennett treats all pets and people with respect, dignity, and compassion. His goal is to provide quality mobile veterinary care in the security of your pet’s own home — eliminating the fear and anxiety that so often come with a trip to the clinic.

Among his colleagues and clients, Dr. Bennett is particularly noted for his ability to calm fractious and fearful cats — pets who may not have tolerated a clinic visit at all.

Why House Calls?

For Dr. Bennett, the choice to practice exclusively in clients’ homes was a deliberate one. A house-call veterinarian can spend real, unhurried time with each patient. There are no waiting rooms, no carriers, no unfamiliar smells or sounds. Both pet and family stay relaxed, which makes for better medicine and a better experience — especially in the most sensitive moments of a pet’s life.
